4. RENTAL ARBITRAGE EXPLANATION
==================================================

What is rental arbitrage?
Rental arbitrage is a model where a property is secured and then monetized through short-term stays at a higher effective income yield than a standard long-term lease.

Simple example:
- Monthly property cost: $1,800
- Nightly rate: $150
- Booked nights: 22 per month
- Monthly revenue: $3,300
- Example operating profit before other considerations: $1,500

This example is educational only. Real performance varies by occupancy, pricing, local demand, operating cost, and season.

5. INVESTAIR INVESTMENT MODEL
==================================================

How does investing on InvestAir work?
InvestAir centers on rental period investing. Investors browse available properties, review estimated performance, select an available duration, fund their account, and reserve a rental-income period for that property.

Typical durations may include:
- 2 months
- 3 months
- 6 months
- 9 months
- 12 months
- 18 months
- 24 months

Important rule:
Durations may vary by property and platform setup.

What is a rental slot?
A rental slot is a defined time window attached to a property. A single slot cannot be assigned to multiple investors at the same time.

Example:
Property: Miami Beach Apartment
Jan 1 - Mar 31 = Slot 1
Apr 1 - Jun 30 = Slot 2
Jul 1 - Sep 30 = Slot 3

If one period is already reserved, the investor may choose the next available period.

10. INVESTMENT CHECKOUT AND INVESTMENT CREATION
==================================================

Frontend checkout page/component copy:
- Investment checkout.
- Choose your duration, preview the occupancy-adjusted earnings, and invest directly from your funded wallet balance.
- Daily payout.
- Occupancy rate.
- Estimated formula.
- Estimated profit = daily payout x 30 days x occupancy % x selected months.

Implemented backend behavior:
- An investment can be created only if the property exists.
- Supported durationMonths values are exactly: 2, 3, 6, 9, 12, 18, 24.
- slotPrice must be greater than zero.
- startDate must be valid.
- endDate must be after startDate.
- If endDate is not provided, the backend calculates it from startDate and durationMonths.
- The backend checks for overlap with existing reserved or active investments on the same property.
- The investment currency is taken from the payload or the property payout currency, defaulting to USDT.
- The user must have a wallet in that currency.
- The wallet must have enough available balance.
- On success, the wallet is debited, locked and reserved balances are increased, and the investment is created.
- The created investment status becomes active if the start date is current or past, otherwise reserved.
- paymentStatus is set to paid.

Compliance-safe chatbot answer:
To invest, choose a property, open checkout, select one of the supported durations, and confirm the investment using the balance in your funded wallet. The system checks property availability, validates the duration, verifies that your wallet has enough available balance, and then reserves or activates the investment slot.

Important limitations:
- Durations are fixed. No custom month count should be promised.
- A slot can fail if it overlaps an existing reserved or active slot.
- The chatbot should not promise that checkout always succeeds even when a user sees a listing.

Common investing questions and answers:

Q: How do I invest?
A: Fund your account first, open a property, click Invest now, choose a supported duration, and confirm the investment if your wallet balance is sufficient.

Q: What durations are supported?
A: The backend supports 2, 3, 6, 9, 12, 18, and 24 months.

Q: What happens when I confirm the investment?
A: The platform checks slot availability and available balance, then debits the wallet and creates the investment record.

Q: Can I invest without funding my wallet?
A: No. The wallet for the required currency must exist and have enough available balance.

Q: What if someone else already reserved the slot?
A: The backend checks for overlapping active or reserved periods and can reject the request if the slot overlaps an existing reservation.
